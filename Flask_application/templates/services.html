How can a person identify deepfakes

  1. Check for inconsistencies: Look for any unnatural distortions, blurriness, or artifacts in the image that may indicate manipulation.
  2. Inspect facial features: Pay close attention to facial details such as eyes, eyebrows, and mouth. In some deepfake images, these features may appear distorted or misaligned.
  3. Review lighting and shadows: Inconsistent lighting or shadows in the image could be a sign of manipulation.
  4. Verify context: Consider the source of the image and the context in which it is presented. Be cautious of images that seem out of place or are accompanied by suspicious captions or stories.
  5. Use reverse image search: Use online tools or search engines to perform a reverse image search and see if the image appears elsewhere on the internet under different contexts.

Our project utilizes Neural Networks, a type of artificial intelligence, to detect deepfake images

  1. Training the Neural Network: We train our neural network using a large dataset of both real and deepfake images. During training, the network learns to distinguish between authentic and manipulated images by identifying patterns and inconsistencies specific to deepfakes.
  2. Feature Extraction: The neural network extracts various features from input images, such as facial landmarks, texture patterns, and pixel-level details.
  3. Deepfake Detection: Based on the extracted features, the neural network analyzes the input image and computes a probability score indicating the likelihood of it being a deepfake. Higher scores suggest a higher probability of manipulation.
  4. Output: The detection results are then presented to the user, indicating whether the input image is likely a deepfake or not.
